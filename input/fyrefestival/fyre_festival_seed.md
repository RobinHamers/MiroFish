# Fyre Festival — Pre-Event Briefing Document (Early April 2017)

## Executive Summary

Fyre Festival is an upcoming luxury music festival created by Billy McFarland (CEO of Fyre Media Inc.) and rapper Ja Rule. It is scheduled for April 28–30 and May 5–7, 2017, on the island of Great Exuma in the Bahamas. The festival was created to promote Fyre Media's talent booking app. Tickets range from $1,200 to over $12,000 for VIP packages including luxury villa accommodations, yacht access, gourmet meals prepared by celebrity chefs, and performances by major musical acts including Blink-182, Major Lazer, and Disclosure.

## Marketing & Promotion

The festival was promoted through an unprecedented social media influencer campaign. Over 400 influencers, models, and celebrities posted coordinated orange-tile Instagram posts on December 12, 2016. Key paid promoters include:

- **Kendall Jenner** — paid $250,000 for a single Instagram post
- **Bella Hadid** — featured in the promotional video
- **Hailey Baldwin** — featured in the promotional video
- **Emily Ratajkowski** — featured in the promotional video

The promotional video showed supermodels swimming with pigs, jet-skiing, and lounging on white sand beaches on a private island, promising "the cultural experience of a decade." None of the influencers disclosed they were paid to promote the event, violating FTC guidelines.

The marketing materials originally featured Norman's Cay, a private Bahamian island with historical connections to the Medellin Drug Cartel's Pablo Escobar. The promotional copy even highlighted these connections as a selling point. When the island's lease owners discovered this, they immediately terminated the lease — just four months before the festival date.

## Venue & Logistics

After losing Norman's Cay, McFarland relocated the festival to a site on Great Exuma near a Sandals Resort. However:

- Marketing materials continued showing the original private island location with digitally altered maps
- Ticket purchasers were never informed of the venue change
- The new site is not a private island but a construction lot adjacent to a commercial resort
- Billy McFarland has no prior experience organizing music festivals or large-scale events
- Industry professionals warned that the event would cost at least $50 million to execute properly and needed an additional year of planning
- The festival was announced only five months before its scheduled date

## Financial Situation

- McFarland raised approximately $26 million from investors using documents that the FBI later described as falsified
- The original catering budget of $6 million was slashed to $1 million just two weeks before the event
- The high-end catering company withdrew due to unpaid bills
- A replacement caterer was hired on extremely short notice with drastically reduced capabilities
- Most of the budget was spent on influencer marketing rather than event infrastructure

## Accommodation Plans

Attendees were promised:
- Luxury villas and eco-friendly geodesic domes
- Private beach access
- Gourmet dining from celebrity chefs
- Concierge service and yacht transfers

Current site preparations suggest:
- FEMA-style disaster relief tents are being erected instead of luxury villas
- No running water or electricity infrastructure has been completed
- Mattresses are being delivered in bulk packaging
- Sanitation facilities are minimal
- No medical personnel have been contracted
- Security arrangements are insufficient for the expected attendance

## Musical Lineup

Confirmed performers include Blink-182, Major Lazer, Disclosure, Migos, Lil Yachty, Tyga, Desiigner, and Kaytranada, among others. However, several acts have expressed concerns about the festival's readiness.

## Key Stakeholders

- **Billy McFarland** (age 25) — Founder and CEO of Fyre Media, primary organizer
- **Ja Rule (Jeffrey Atkins)** — Co-founder and public face of the festival
- **Fyre Media Inc.** — Parent company, also developing the Fyre talent booking app
- **Investors** — Multiple venture capital and private investors totaling ~$26 million
- **Ticket holders** — Thousands of attendees, many having paid $1,200–$12,000+
- **Bahamian workers and suppliers** — Local contractors hired for construction and services
- **Influencers and models** — 400+ social media promoters
- **Musical artists and their management teams**

## Risk Factors

1. Venue was changed without informing ticket holders
2. Organizer has zero event management experience
3. Budget has been primarily allocated to marketing, not infrastructure
4. Key vendors have withdrawn due to non-payment
5. Timeline is critically compressed (5 months for an event experts say needs 18+)
6. No proper permits, safety inspections, or medical personnel arranged
7. Remote island location complicates supply chains and emergency response
8. Investor documents may contain material misrepresentations
